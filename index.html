• 1910 - Born at Skopje, North Macedonia.
  • 1919 - Her father died while she was eight years old.
  • 1928 - Left home at age 18 to join the Sisters of Loreto at Loreto Abbey in Rathfarnham, Ireland, to learn English with the intent of becoming a missionary.
  • 1931 - Took her first religious vows.
  • 1937 - Took her solemn vows while she was a teacher at the Loreto convent school in Entally, eastern Calcutta.
  • 1944 - Appointed as the headmistress in that school. Although Teresa enjoyed teaching at the school, she was increasingly disturbed by the poverty surrounding her in Calcutta.
  • 1946 - Direct Action Day began a period of Muslim-Hindu violence and at that time Teresa experienced "the call within the call" when she traveled by train to the Loreto convent in Darjeeling from Calcutta for her annual retreat.
  • 1948 - Began her missionary work with the poor replacing her traditional Loreto habit with a simple, white cotton sari with a blue border.
  • 1950 - Founded â€˜Missionaries of Charity' which would care for "the hungry, the naked, the homeless, the crippled, the blind, the lepers, all those people who feel unwanted, unloved, uncared for throughout society, people that have become a burden to the society and are shunned by everyone" in her words.
  • 1952 - Opened her first hospice with help from Calcutta officials and converted an abandoned Hindu temple into the Kalighat Home for the Dying, free for the poor, and renamed it Kalighat, the Home of the Pure Heart (Nirmal Hriday).
  • 1955 - Opened Nirmala Shishu Bhavan, the Children's Home of the Immaculate Heart, as a haven for orphans and homeless youth.
  • 1962 - Received the Ramon Magsaysay Award for Peace and International Understanding, given for work in South or East Asia and at the same year, she received Padma Shri.
  • 1971 - Pope Paul VI gave her the inaugural Pope John XXIII Peace Prize commending her work with the poor, display of Christian charity and efforts for peace.
  • 1976 - In the month of April, Teresa visited the University of Scranton in northeastern Pennsylvania, where she received the La Storta Medal for Human Service from university president William J. Byron.
  • 1979 - Mother Teresa received the Nobel Peace Prize "for work undertaken in the struggle to overcome poverty and distress, which also constitutes a threat to peace.
  • 1981 - Founded the Corpus Christi Movement for Priests and after three years, she founded the Missionaries of Charity Fathers with Joseph Langford, to combine the vocational aims of the Missionaries of Charity with the resources of the priesthood.
  • 1987 - In the month of August, Teresa received an honorary doctor of social science degree, in recognition of her service and her ministry to help the destitute and sick, from the university of Scranton where she spoke to over 4,000 students and members of the Diocese of Scranton about her service to the "poorest of the poor", telling them to "do small things with great love".
  • 1992 - Her official biography was published by Navin Chawla.
  • 1997 - Teresa resigned as the head of the Missionaries of Charity and in the month of September, she passed away.
